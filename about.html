About Me

I study how managers select and use data when they pursue responsible digital transformation goals. My work sits between data governance, sustainability, and ethics. I'm interested in how organizations claim responsibility — and what that actually looks like in practice.

I also explore how we can turn research ideas into practical tools, lightweight prototypes, and decision support for real people inside organizations.

Research Interests

• Responsible Digital Transformation • Data Governance & Accountability • Sustainability and Digital Ethics • Managerial decision-making with data • Translating academic thinking into tools people actually use

Methodologically I combine qualitative inquiry (interviews with managers, decision-makers, responsible AI people, etc.) with conceptual framing from information systems, governance, and sustainability studies.

My core question: Who decides what “responsible” means, and what evidence (data) is considered legitimate in that decision?
