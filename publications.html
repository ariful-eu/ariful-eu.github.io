Publications & Work in Progress

Below are selected research directions and works in progress. My focus is on responsible digital transformation: how organizations justify, govern, and operationalize “responsibility” when they adopt data-driven technologies.

The Role of Data in Responsible Digital Transformation

Research-in-progress. How do managers select and use data when they pursue responsible digital transformation goals? This work analyzes how decision-makers define “responsibility,” what counts as acceptable evidence, and how tensions emerge between ethics, sustainability, and efficiency.

Target: Information Systems / digital responsibility venues.

Governance, Accountability, and Data Use in Sustainability Transitions

Conceptual work linking digital governance, sustainability reporting, and managerial accountability. Explores how “responsible AI / responsible digitalization” is being translated into frameworks, dashboards, and audit trails inside organizations.

Status: conceptual framing, qualitative data collection planned.

From Policy Talk to Practice: How Responsibility Gets Operationalized

Early work examining how “responsibility” (ethics, social impact, ESG-type language) moves from strategy documents into concrete data selection criteria, KPIs, and risk decisions.

Status: draft interview protocol, seeking more managerial voices.
